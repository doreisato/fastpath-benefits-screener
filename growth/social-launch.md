# Social Media Launch Plan

**Core Thesis:** Poverty and benefits are high-emotion, high-utility topics. Content must be hyper-practical, empathy-driven, and slightly antagonistic toward the broken bureaucratic system.

## Platform 1: TikTok & YouTube Shorts (Primary Acquisition)
**Format:** POV/Screen-recording style. High energy, utility first.
**Schedule:** 1 post per day for 7 days.
**Content Concepts:**
1. **The Hook:** "Did you know you could be missing out on $291/month for groceries?" -> **The Pitch:** Show the calculator working in 60 seconds.
2. **The Hack:** "The one deduction that gets people approved for SNAP." -> Explain housing/childcare deductions. Link calculator in bio.
3. **The Target:** "If you are a gig worker (Uber/Doordash), watch this." -> Explain how net income works for 1099s regarding food stamps.
4. **The Enemy:** "Why state websites make it so hard to check your benefits." -> Contrast the clunky IL state site with our fast, clean MVP.

## Platform 2: Facebook Groups (Hyper-Local Acquisition)
**Format:** Plain text, highly authentic, no marketing speak.
**Schedule:** Manual posting in 5-10 local groups.
**Targets:** "Chicago Moms", "Illinois Unemployment Support", "Buy Nothing [Neighborhood]".
**Content:**
"Hey neighbors, I know groceries are crazy expensive right now. A lot of people don't realize the income limits for food stamps went up. I built a quick calculator to check if you qualify without having to enter your SSN or email. Hope this helps someone out."

## Platform 3: X/Twitter (Build-in-Public / Tech Acquisition)
**Format:** Threads.
**Schedule:** Launch Day thread, Day 3 update, Day 7 metrics.
**Content:**
*   **Launch Thread:** Focus on the broken safety net. "Millions of Americans are eligible for food stamps but don't apply because the state websites look like they were built in 1998. So we fixed it. Introducing Benefits Navigator MVP."
*   Break down the tech stack, the UX decisions (no PII required), and the social mission.
*   Tag civic tech influencers, Code for America, and local politicians.

## Success Metrics for Week 1
*   10,000 unique calculator runs.
*   10% click-through rate to the official state application portal.
*   3 positive replies from partner outreach.
