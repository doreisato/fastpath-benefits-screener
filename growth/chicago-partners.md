# Chicago Anchor Partners (B2B2C Distribution)

**Core Thesis:** Social workers and food banks already have distribution. Give them a tool that saves them time, and they will distribute it to their clients for us.

## Food Banks & Pantries
1. **Greater Chicago Food Depository** (The central hub - ultimate target)
2. **Nourishing Hope** (Formerly Lakeview Pantry - highly organized, tech-forward)
3. **Beyond Hunger** (Oak Park/West Side - excellent community integration)
4. **Care for Real** (Edgewater/Rogers Park)
5. **A Just Harvest** (Rogers Park)
6. **Common Pantry** (North Center)

## Healthcare & Community Health Clinics
7. **Howard Brown Health** (Massive footprint, heavy social services component)
8. **Erie Family Health Centers** (Bilingual, high volume of Medicaid/SNAP eligible patients)
9. **Heartland Alliance** (Major social services provider)
10. **Esperanza Health Centers** (Southwest side, predominantly Latino demographic)
11. **The Night Ministry** (Street medicine, unhoused population)

## Broad Social Services & Housing
12. **Catholic Charities of the Archdiocese of Chicago** (Massive volume of intake)
13. **Metropolitan Family Services** (Deep community roots)
14. **Inspiration Corporation** (Focus on homelessness and poverty)
15. **Connections for the Homeless** (Evanston/North Shore)
16. **Center on Halsted** (LGBTQ+ community center with dedicated social services)

## Specialized & Mutual Aid
17. **Cradles to Crayons Chicago** (Focus on children, connects to family services)
18. **Chicago Mutual Aid Network** (Grassroots, highly trusted by the community)
19. **Inner-City Muslim Action Network (IMAN)** (South/Southwest side)
20. **Family Focus** (Early childhood and family support services)

**Outreach Tactic:** Do not sell. Ask for feedback.
*Subject:* "Quick question about your intake process for SNAP"
*Body:* "Hi [Name], I'm a local dev who built a 60-second anonymous SNAP calculator. I'm trying to figure out if this would save your case workers time during intake. Can I show you a 30-second demo? It's completely free, just want to make it useful."
