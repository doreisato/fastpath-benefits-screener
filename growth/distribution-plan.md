# Distribution Plan: Launch Week

**Core Thesis:** Zero CAC. Pure organic distribution through existing high-trust networks and algorithmic arbitrage.

## Phase 1: Community Seeding (Days 1-2)
**Goal:** Prove utility, get initial feedback, build word-of-mouth.
*   **Reddit Strategy:**
    *   `r/povertyfinance`: Post title: "Built a free, no-BS SNAP eligibility calculator that doesn't require personal info. Figured it might help here." (Focus on privacy and speed).
    *   `r/foodstamps`: Engage with top "do I qualify" posts from the last 7 days. Reply with specific calculations from our tool, then link it.
    *   `r/chicago` & `r/illinois`: Localized drop. "The state site is confusing, so I built a 60-second Illinois benefits checker."
    *   `r/personalfinance`: Mention in relevant daily threads regarding budgeting on low income.
*   **Hacker News:** "Show HN: Benefits Navigator MVP - A fast, private SNAP eligibility calculator." Focus on the engineering and social impact.

## Phase 2: SEO Indexing & Programmatic Launch (Days 3-4)
**Goal:** Capture high-intent search traffic.
*   Deploy 50 state-specific landing pages (e.g., `/calculator/illinois`, `/calculator/texas`).
*   Submit sitemaps to Google Search Console immediately.
*   Push internal linking structure from the homepage to states to pass authority.
*   Write 3 high-value core guides: "How to calculate SNAP income", "Deductions you're missing for EBT", "Emergency SNAP qualifications".

## Phase 3: Partner Outreach (Days 5-7)
**Goal:** Secure institutional distribution.
*   Execute cold email sequence to the 20 Chicago partner list.
*   **Offer:** A free, co-branded URL for their case workers (e.g., `benefitsnavigator.com/partner/catholic-charities`).
*   **Value prop:** Save social workers 15 minutes per intake by having clients pre-screen themselves in the waiting room via QR code.

## Ongoing: Viral Mechanics
*   Add a "Share my anonymous results" feature (generates an image card of eligibility to share on Facebook/Twitter).
*   Add "Send to a friend who might need this" CTA after a positive eligibility check.
